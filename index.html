Our Mission

Access to Readiness Should Never Be a Question of Resources

The BRSA Foundation funds direct access to behavioral readiness evaluation and certification programs for underserved individuals — those who cannot otherwise afford the assessment, documentation, and support services that determine their eligibility for housing, employment, and civic participation.

We operate independently from the Legacyline platform and BRSA Holdings. We hold no technology assets and exercise no control over standards. Our sole mandate is to ensure that financial status is never a barrier to behavioral readiness certification.

Where We Work

Six Sectors, One Standard of Readiness

The Foundation directs funding to individuals navigating critical life transitions across sectors where behavioral readiness determines outcomes.

01

Housing Readiness

Supporting individuals seeking stable housing who require behavioral readiness certification for landlord, lender, or program eligibility.

Individual Readiness
02

Workforce Development

Funding readiness evaluation for job seekers whose employment prospects depend on documented behavioral competencies.

Economic Mobility
03

Corrections Reentry

Partnering with reentry programs to provide certification pathways for justice-involved individuals re-entering society.

Second Chance
04

Education Access

Removing certification cost barriers for students and adults whose enrollment or academic standing requires readiness documentation.

Academic Continuity
05

Economic Development

Supporting community-based economic initiatives that require workforce behavioral readiness as a program standard.

Community Investment
06

Institutional Partnerships

Working with lenders, housing authorities, and employers to establish grant-funded readiness tracks within their programs.

Systemic Change

How It Works

From Grant to Certification

1

Referral & Intake

Individuals are referred through partner organizations or apply directly. A brief intake confirms eligibility and need.

2

Grant Award

The Foundation reviews the referral and issues a grant covering evaluation fees and associated documentation costs.

3

BRSA Evaluation

A certified BRSA evaluator conducts the behavioral assessment through the Legacyline platform.

4

Certification Issued

Upon successful evaluation, the individual receives a BRSA Readiness Certification usable across housing, workforce, and partner institutions.
